Asset: An asset is a resource with economic value that an individual, corporation, or country owns or controls with the expectation that it will provide a future benefit. Assets are reported on a company's balance sheet and they're classified as current, financial, and intangible. They are bought or created to increase a firm's value or benefit the firm's operation. 
Audit: Audit refers to the review of financial statements. A financial audit is an objective examination and evaluation of the financial statements of an organization to make sure that the financial records are a fair and accurate represetnation of the transactions they claim to represent. 
Debit
    Def: Debit is an accounting entry that results in either an increase in assets or a decrease in liabilities on a company's balance sheet. In double-entry bookkeeping, all debits are made on the left side of the ledger and must be offset with corresponding credits on the right side of the ledger.
Grant
    Def: A grant is a gift to an individual or a company that does not need to be paid back. Grants usually involve a three-phase lifecycle: pre-award, award, and post-award. Grants are considered income and thus subject to taxation, unless the law says otherwise.
Index
    Def: An index in measures the price performance of a basket of securities using a standardized metric and methodology. Indexes in financial markets are often used as benchmarks to evaluate an investment's performacne against.
